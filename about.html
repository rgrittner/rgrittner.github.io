My Teaching Philosophy

Our dogs are faced with many choices in their everyday lives. They can run out the door or wait patiently, pull on the leash towards a fascinating smell or choose to walk alongside their handler. My training methods focus on the dog's decision making skills while teaching the handler how to create situations that set them both up for success. Each decision the dog makes is met with a very clear and direct response from the handler which informs the dog about the decision they made, enabling a deeper understanding of behaviors.



About Me

When I was 17 years old I became a dog trainer by default when I got my first border collie, Cindy. Discovering competitive obedience with Cindy sparked my life-long love of dog training. I entered my first obedience trial in 2006 and earned a High In Trial (the highest score of all entered dogs). Since then, my dogs and I also earned titles in agility, obedience, rally, and herding. As a life-long student I regularly participate in on-line training programs from Susan Garrett, attend seminars with Ann Braue and Susan Salo, and am a regular student at Think Pawsitive with my young dog Ender. While I enjoy participating in dog shows, my primary goal is to train dogs to be well mannered family pets. It just so happens that the skills that really make a great family pet are the foundations to excel in competitive sports. I offer private lessons and group classes in my back yard and basement training facility.



Outside of my dog training I am the mother of a very active two year old boy, enjoy board gaming with my husband, and venture out to the barn where my horse Levi and I are learning the art of Hunter/Jumpers.
